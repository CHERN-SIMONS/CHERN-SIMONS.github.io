The results of Mercury are so limited that we can hardly use it to restrain $a_{ng}/a_{gal}$.

However, looking at the formula of advance, one can see that actually, contract to GR's result, the result is larger and larger, as distance between one planet and Sun, while GR states that it would be smaller and smaller. $a\uparrow\Longrightarrow \Delta\omega_5\uparrow,~\Delta\omega_{GR}\downarrow$ $$ \begin{align} \Delta\varpi_5 = & -\frac{2\eta_{DM}a_g\pi a^2}{Gm e^3}\big[1+e^4-\sqrt{1-e^2}-2e^2(1-\sqrt{1-e^2}) \big] \\ &~\times \bigg[ \cos(\Phi-\Omega)\cos\omega\sin\Theta+\cos\Theta\sin\iota\sin\omega + \sin\omega\cos\iota\sin\Theta\sin(\Phi-\Omega) \bigg] \ & - \frac{\eta_{DM}a_g \pi a^2}{e^3Gm}\sqrt{1-e^2}\bigg[ 2-e^2-2\sqrt{1-e^2}+2e^2\sqrt{1-e^2} \bigg]\ &~\times\bigg[ \cos\Theta\sin\iota\sin\omega+\cos\iota\sin\Theta\sin\omega\sin(\Phi-\Omega)+\sin\Theta\cos\omega\cos(\Phi-\Omega) \bigg]\ & + \frac{3\eta_{DM}a_g\pi a^2}{Gm\sqrt{1-e^2}}(\cos\iota-1)\sin\omega[\cos\Theta\cot\iota - \sin\Theta\sin(\Phi-\Omega)]\ \Delta\omega_{GR} = & \frac{6\pi Gm}{ac^2(1-e^2)}\left[\frac{1}{3}(2+2\gamma-\beta)+\frac{1}{6}\eta(2\alpha_1-\alpha_2+\alpha_3+2\xi_2)+\frac{J_2R^2c^2}{2Gma(1-e^2)} \right] \end{align} $$ For Saturn $$ \Delta\varpi_5 = 32.4\eta_{DM},,\Delta\omega_{GR}=1.359\times 10^{-2}~\text{as/cty},,\Delta\varpi \simeq 4\times10^{-4}\text{as/cty} $$ For Mars $$ \Delta\varpi_5 = (28.3\cos\omega-19.2\sin\omega)\eta_{DM},,\Delta\omega_{GR} = 1.351~~\text{as/cty} $$ For Jupiter $$ \Delta\varpi_5 = (4.9\cos\omega-123.0\sin\omega)\eta_{DM},,\Delta\omega_{GR} = 6.236\times 10^{-2}\text{as/cty} $$

1/4/2019

Relativistic Doppler effect, for moving observers and source, is $$ \begin{align} \frac{f_o}{f_s} = \frac{1-\frac{|v_o|}{c}\cos\theta_{c,v_o}}{1-\frac{|v_s|}{c}\cos\theta_{c,v_s}}\sqrt{\frac{1-(v_o/c)^2}{1-(v_s/c)^2}}\approx 1+\frac{(|v_s|\cos\theta_{c,v_s}-|v_o|\cos\theta_{c,v_o})}{c} \end{align} $$ with $v_s/c \sim 10^{-4}$.

This frequency could be seen by Sun. From the point of view of Earth, it should be $$ f_o = \frac{f_s}{\gamma (1+\beta \cos\theta_o)} $$ with $$ \begin{align} w =& \frac{\sqrt{u^2+v^2-2uv\cos\theta-(v^2u^2\sin^2\theta)/c^2}}{1-\frac{uv}{c^2}\cos\theta} \ = & |\vec{u}-\vec{v}|+\frac{1}{c^2}\bigg[ uv\cos\theta|\vec{u}-\vec{v}|-\frac{u^2v^2\sin^2\theta}{2|\vec{u}-\vec{v}|} \bigg] +\mathcal{O}(c^{-4})\ \beta = & \frac{w}{c}\ \tan\theta'= &\frac{\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}u\sin\theta}{u\cos\theta-v} \end{align} $$

$$ v^2 = \dot{r}^2+r^2\dot{\theta}^2 $$
